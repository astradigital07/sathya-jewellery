The Beginning

From a Temple Workshop to a Trusted Name

In 1985, in a narrow lane behind the Madurai Meenakshi temple, a young goldsmith named Sathya Ramanathan set up a modest workshop. His first commission was a small gold kavacham for the temple deity — a piece so flawlessly crafted that word spread within weeks. Families began arriving at his door, not merely for ornaments, but for jewellery that would mark their most sacred occasions.

What began as one man's devotion to his craft slowly became a family legacy. His son, Karthik, returned from training in Jaipur and Antwerp to introduce diamond-setting and contemporary design while preserving the soul of traditional South Indian jewellery. Today, the third generation — Ananya, a gemologist from London — carries forward the same commitment to purity, artistry, and trust.

Every Sathya piece still passes through human hands, not just machines. The hammer, the blowpipe, the polishing wheel — they remain the heart of our atelier, just as they were in 1985.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Uncompromising Purity

Every gram of gold is BIS hallmarked and every diamond is IGI certified. We have never compromised on material integrity — and we never will.

Ethical Sourcing

Our gold is fair-trade refined and our diamonds are Kimberley-certified conflict-free. Beauty should never come at the cost of conscience.

Artisan Dignity

Our karigars are not labour — they are custodians of craft. We pay fair wages, offer healthcare, and pass on a share of every bespoke commission.

Lifetime Trust

From lifetime exchange to free annual polishing, our relationship with you does not end at the bill — it begins there.

Sustainable Practice

We recycle 70% of our water, use solar-fired furnaces, and have eliminated single-use plastics across all six boutiques.

Design Integrity

We design in-house, never copy, and refuse to chase trends. Every collection is conceived to be worn — and inherited — for decades.
